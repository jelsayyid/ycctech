developer prize 2026
$1,000

build the best tool for the yale community.

three cash prizes, plus gift cards for every meaningful submission. judged by faculty and tech leaders on impact, usability, openness. ceremony at tsai city on apr 25.

breakdown

$1,000 across three tiers

  • 1st — $500 grand prize for the top project.
  • 2nd — $300 runner-up.
  • 3rd — $200 third place.
  • every meaningful submission also gets a gift card.
process

four steps

  • 01 build: new project, started after the announcement. solo or teams up to 3.
  • 02 ship: public github repo. must serve the yale community directly.
  • 03 submit: 2-min demo video + short problem write-up, by deadline.
  • 04 ceremony: catered event at tsai city. $1,000 awarded.
criteria

what judges look for

weighted in order. simple and well-executed beats overengineered.

01

community impact

does this solve a real problem for yale students? how many would actually use it? would they tell their friends?

02

usability & polish

usable right now, not a demo. shipped beats ambitious and half-finished.

03

openness

open-source. extensible. strengthens the broader yale dev ecosystem.

04

technical quality

clean code, thoughtful decisions. not complexity for its own sake.
